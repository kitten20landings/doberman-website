The Doberman is a strong, muscular dog with a robust build and an expressive silhouette. It is also called the "aristocrat dog"

It has such an aristocratic look thanks to its long neck, elongated head, strong hips and a taut belly. The coat of this breed is smooth and silky.
It emphasizes every muscle of the Doberman very clearly.

The gait in these dogs is light, as if flying. The color is allowed black and dark brown with reddish tan. Thanks to their great endurance, sharp intellect and strong loyalty to their owner, Dobermans have an unlimited number of admirers around the world.

Care

Dobermans need a balanced diet that includes a large amount of boiled or fresh and scalded meat, cereals, vegetables, vitamins. An improperly selected diet can lead to a deterioration in the appearance and well-being of the animal, and the appearance of health problems.

Doberman hygiene

The smooth, dense coat practically does not get dirty, so you should not bathe your pet often. It is enough to wash it 1-2 times a year with a special shampoo (you can use a shampoo with the effect of destroying fleas and other ectoparasites). If the dog gets very dirty while walking, its paws and stomach can be wiped with a damp cloth. An important hygienic procedure is cleaning the ears, which must be wiped at least once every 2 weeks with a cotton pad.

Maintenance in the apartment

Smooth-haired dogs do not need daily brushing, it is enough to comb them with a massage brush at least once a week. Claws are trimmed as they grow (unless the dog grinds them down). Dental care is also important.
